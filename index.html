A Recent History of UFOs

the possibility of unidentified flying objects in 2020

News outlets in 2020 have been overwhelmed by the coronavirus pandemic, historical protests, and a presidential election. But one less conventional topic has made headlines in the sea of news: unidentified flying objects.

A recent New York Times article focused on a Senate Committee report, which revealed yearly spending records by U.S. intelligence agencies. Among the programs is the Unidentified Aerial Phenomenon Task Force, created “to standardize collection and reporting” on UFO sightings, and to publicly release at least some information within 180 days of passing the Intelligence Authorization Act for the 2021 fiscal year.

While many hope the program will publicize evidence of extraterrestrial visitors, the stated reason of the program is to discover any potential danger posed by a breakthrough in aviation technology by foreign nations.

This news comes soon after the Pentagon confirmed that footage captured of an apparent UFO by U.S. fighter planes is in fact, real. It seems the existence of UFOs has become increasingly normalized in the past several decades. Words that once sounded like a tinfoil-hat conspiracy are now being written by government agencies and major news outlets. And in times like these, UFOs may as well be the least of our worries.
